  • Indian cuisine restaurant menus differ from other cuisines in several ways. Firstly, Indian menus often feature a variety of vegetarian and vegan options due to cultural and religious practices. Dishes like dal, paneer, and aloo gobi are common. Secondly, Indian cuisine is known for its extensive use of spices and flavors, with dishes like biryani, curry, and masala. The use of rice and bread (like naan and roti) as staple food is also a distinctive feature..
  • Typical Japanese recipes include soy sauce, miso, and umeboshi (pickled plums) rather than red meat, oils and fats, and dairy products. Even though the low-sodium versions are available, these commonly used ingredients have high salt content. Nevertheless, Japanese cuisine may be considered as healthy.
  • Meat, fish, and rice are the staple foods of Emirati cuisine. Lamb and mutton are the more favored meats rather than goat, beef, and camel meat. Dates are usually consumed with meals.

This type of cusines and all serving with tradition and culture.
